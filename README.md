# The Unknown Experiment

> A public social experiment exploring trust, anonymity, transparency and voluntary support between strangers.

---

# Introduction

The Unknown Experiment is an independent public initiative created to explore a simple but difficult question:

## Can strangers voluntarily support another stranger when there is:
- no reward,
- no emergency,
- no obligation,
- no investment opportunity,
- and no personal relationship?

This project is intentionally minimal, transparent and anonymous.

It is not built around fame, influence, emotional manipulation or promises of profit.

The objective is not to “sell” a story.

The objective is to observe human behavior honestly.

---

# Why This Experiment Exists

Modern internet culture is heavily driven by:
- advertising,
- personal branding,
- influencer culture,
- emotional storytelling,
- financial incentives,
- speculation,
- and algorithmic attention.

Most online support systems depend on:
- rewards,
- social recognition,
- entertainment,
- pity,
- investment expectations,
- or community pressure.

The Unknown Experiment attempts to remove most of those elements.

This project asks a direct question:

## What happens when a completely unknown person openly asks for voluntary support with radical honesty and no promises in return?

---

# Core Principles

## 1. Anonymity

The creator intentionally remains anonymous.

This is not done to deceive people, but to:
- preserve personal privacy,
- avoid personality cults,
- reduce bias,
- and keep attention focused on the experiment itself rather than the individual behind it.

The creator is publicly described only as:
> “An anonymous individual based in Central Africa.”

---

## 2. Transparency

The rules of the experiment are public.

The website, public wallet address, statistics and reports are openly accessible.

Participation methods are visible to everyone equally.

No hidden promises exist.

---

## 3. No Investment

This project is NOT:
- an investment,
- a crowdfunding startup,
- a token project,
- a financial product,
- or a profit-sharing system.

No financial return is promised or implied.

Participants should never expect:
- profit,
- ownership,
- dividends,
- privileges,
- or future compensation.

---

## 4. No Manipulation

The experiment rejects:
- fake emergencies,
- fabricated stories,
- fake testimonials,
- emotional pressure,
- false scarcity,
- and deceptive marketing tactics.

No attempt is made to guilt people into participating.

---

## 5. Freedom

Every person is completely free to:
- participate,
- observe,
- question,
- criticize,
- ignore,
- or leave.

Non-participation is fully respected.

---

# The Research Objective

The experiment explores several social and psychological questions:

- How do strangers react to radical honesty online?
- Can transparency create trust without identity?
- Will people voluntarily support an unknown individual?
- Does anonymity reduce or increase credibility?
- How much of internet support depends on emotional storytelling?
- What happens if nothing is promised in return?

The experiment accepts all outcomes equally:
- success,
- failure,
- silence,
- criticism,
- curiosity,
- or indifference.

All are considered meaningful observations.

---

# Public Reporting

The experiment is designed to evolve publicly.

Updates may include:
- public observations,
- participation statistics,
- blockchain activity,
- reflections,
- and social reactions.

Both positive and negative results may be documented.

If the experiment fails completely, that failure will also be publicly acknowledged.

---

# Voluntary Participation

Participation is entirely voluntary.

No person is individually targeted or pressured.

Anyone considering participation should first understand:
- the Transparency Charter,
- the absence of rewards,
- and the experimental nature of the initiative.
