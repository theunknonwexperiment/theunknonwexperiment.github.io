About the experiment

The Unknown Experiment is a public social experiment exploring trust, curiosity, transparency and voluntary support between strangers.

Anonymous by design

The creator remains anonymous to preserve privacy and keep the focus on the experiment rather than the individual behind it.

No promise

This is not an investment, not a charity campaign, not a product and not a financial opportunity.

Public observation

Success or failure will both be documented honestly. A lack of participation is also a valid result.

Transparency Charter

This charter defines the rules of The Unknown Experiment. It exists to make the project clear, honest and understandable before any participation.

Article 1 — Purpose

The purpose of The Unknown Experiment is to observe whether strangers are willing to voluntarily support another stranger without reward, emergency, obligation or personal relationship.

Article 2 — Voluntary participation

Participation is entirely voluntary. No person is required, pressured or individually targeted to contribute.

Article 3 — No compensation

Contributors receive no product, service, reward, privilege, access, ownership, voting right or compensation of any kind.

Article 4 — Not an investment

The Unknown Experiment is not an investment opportunity. No profit, interest, dividend, token, return or financial benefit is promised or implied.

Article 5 — Transparency

The progress of the experiment will be reported publicly through updates, statistics and observations. The objective is to document what happens honestly.

Article 6 — Use of funds

Any funds received may be used by the anonymous creator to support personal or professional activities. No specific use is guaranteed.

Article 7 — Privacy

The creator chooses to remain anonymous to protect personal privacy. The experiment focuses on trust between strangers, not on the public identity of the creator.

Article 8 — No manipulation

No fabricated stories, false emergencies, fake testimonials, misleading identities or emotional manipulation will be used to encourage participation.

Article 9 — Right to ignore

Every visitor is free to participate, follow, question, criticize, observe or ignore the experiment. Non-participation is fully respected.

Article 10 — Failure is valid

The experiment may fail. If no one participates, or if participation remains low, that outcome will be treated as a valid result and documented openly.
